Momo

Description

Momo is a type of steamed filled dumpling, with origins from Tibet. Momo is native to Tibet, Nepal as well as Indian Himalayan Region of Ladakh, Sikkim, Assam, and Arunachal Pradesh and Darjeeling. It is popular accros a wider region of the Indian subcontinent. Momos dumpling are most commonly filled with chicken, pork and goat. It has become a staple delicacy in Nepal and Tibetan commnuities in Bhutan, as well people of the Indian regions of Darjeeling.

Ingredients

Momo Fillings

  • 1kg ground pork
  • 200g Cabbage finely chopped
  • 2 bunches Scallions finely chopped, white part only
  • 1 thumbsized ginger - minced
  • 2 1/2tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon tumeric powder

Dough

  • 6 cups flour (white, all purpose or whole wheat
  • 3 1/2warm water

Sauce

  • 500g tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon plum powder - lapsiko dhulo
  • 1 medium sized red onion - sliced
  • 1 bunch coriander - rougly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds, toasted and ground
  • 2 tablespoon black pepper powder
  • 1 tablespoon chilli powder
  • 3 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2teaspoon salt
  • 11/2 tablespoon water

Instructions

  1. Prepare filling ingredients, dumpling dough, and tomato sauce by following the recipe shown below.
  2. Divide the dumpling dough into roughly 12 individual balls.
  3. Roll them out into 1-inch thick logs, cut each log into 6-8 pieces. Press each piece into thick coins by the palm of your hand.
  4. Roll each coin into round shapes of 3-inch diameter and 1/4cm thick.
  5. Meanwhile, fill the dumpling pot with 2-3 inches of water. Start boiling them.
  6. Fill each rolled dough with 1/2tablespoon of filling. Moisten the edges with water, shape them how you desire, seal the edges, and place them on a greased steamer basket or floured surface until ready to cook.
  7. Steam dumplings for 20 minutes in high heat.
  8. Serve momos with hog plum and tomato sauce.
